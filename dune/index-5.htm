Dune: House Atreides wins the Dragon Award

The collected issues of Dune: House Atreides, volume 2, from BOOM! Studiosscript by Brian Herbert and Kevin J. Anderson, art by Dev Premanik and Alex Guimaraeswon the Dragon Award in the Best Graphic Novel category. Voting for the Dragon Award is open to all fans worldwide, not just attendees of DragonCon, and over 12,000 people cast their votes. Kevin J. Anderson was there to receive the award, a gorgeous blown-glass trophy presented at a large ceremony.


Shai-Hulud Marches in DragonCon Parade

A Dune costuming group went all out for this years DragonCon, participating in the parade as Fremen, Reverend Mothers and even a giant sandworm like a Chinese dragon moving along the parade route.


Dune: House Atreides comics nominated for Dragon Award

The second volume of the collected monthly House Atreides comics from BOOM! Studios has just been nominated for the Dragon Award in the Graphic Novel category. Brian and Kevin adapted their novel into comic form, with art by Dev Pramanik. The Dragon Award is one of the largest awards in science fiction and fantasy, and is presented each year at DragonCon in Atlanta.

Dune Graphic Novel volume 2

The second volume in the definitive graphic novel adaptation of Frank Herberts Dune has just been released by Abrams Books. Dune: Volume 2, MuadDib was scripted by Brian Herbert and Kevin J. Anderson, art by Ral Allen and Patrcia Martn, and a stunning cover by Bill Sienkewicz.

The first volume of the Dune graphic novel was released in 2020, became a New York Times bestseller, won several awards, and was named on Best Graphic Novel of the Year lists.

Brian and Kevin have already delivered the script for the third and final volume of the Dune adaptation, and the artists are hard at work for a planned release in early 2024.

Dune: The Waters of Kanlyrevenge on the sands

BOOM! studios has published a four-issue story arc of a standalone tale set during the time of the novel Dune, The Waters of Kanly. This miniseries, adapted from a short story by Brian Herbert and Kevin J. Anderson, tells the lost years of Gurney Halleck after he fell in among the smugglers following the disastrous Battle of Arrakeen. Gurney plans a terrible revenge against Beast Rabban.
Art by Francesco Mortarino.
